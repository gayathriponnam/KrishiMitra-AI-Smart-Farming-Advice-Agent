IRRIGATION & WATER MANAGEMENT GUIDE
Source: ICAR-IARI, NWPCA, Ministry of Jal Shakti
Last Updated: 2024

=== CROP WATER REQUIREMENTS (mm/season) ===
Rice (lowland):       1200 - 1400 mm
Rice (upland):        800 - 1000 mm
Wheat:                450 - 650 mm
Cotton:               600 - 750 mm  
Sugarcane:            1500 - 2500 mm
Maize:                500 - 600 mm
Soybean:              450 - 550 mm
Chickpea:             250 - 350 mm
Mustard:              300 - 400 mm
Groundnut:            500 - 600 mm
Sunflower:            500 - 600 mm
Tomato:               400 - 600 mm
Potato:               500 - 700 mm

=== IRRIGATION METHODS ===

1. FLOOD/FURROW IRRIGATION
   - Efficiency: 40-50% (very low water use efficiency)
   - Suitable for: Rice, sugarcane, heavy soils
   - Issues: Waterlogging, salt accumulation, weed growth

2. SPRINKLER IRRIGATION
   - Efficiency: 70-80%
   - Suitable for: Wheat, vegetables, groundnut, pulses, uneven terrain
   - Avoid: Fungus-prone crops during humid weather (disease promotion)
   - Cost: Rs. 20,000-60,000/acre (subsidy available under PMKSY)

3. DRIP IRRIGATION
   - Efficiency: 90-95% (highest water use efficiency)
   - Suitable for: Vegetables, fruits, cotton, sugarcane, chilli, onion
   - Saves: 30-50% water compared to flood irrigation
   - Fertilizer: Fertigation (applying fertilizer through drip) saves 20-30% fertilizer
   - Government: 55-85% subsidy under PMKSY (varies by state and farmer category)
   - Drip laterals: 16mm lateral with 4L/hr drippers at 30cm x 60cm spacing (vegetables)

=== IRRIGATION SCHEDULING ===

CRITICAL GROWTH STAGES (Do NOT miss irrigation):
- Rice: Transplanting, active tillering, panicle initiation, heading, grain filling
- Wheat: CRI (Crown Root Initiation), tillering, jointing, flowering, grain fill
- Cotton: Flowering, boll development (most critical)
- Maize: Knee height, tasseling, silking (most critical), grain fill
- Chickpea: Pre-flowering, pod filling (if rainfall inadequate)

SOIL MOISTURE MONITORING:
- Feel method: Squeeze soil; fine sandy loam is ready for irrigation when it barely
  holds shape and drops at waist height
- Tensiometer: Irrigate when reading exceeds 40-50 cbar (sandy) or 60-70 cbar (clay)
- IW/CPE ratio method: Irrigate when IW/CPE ratio reaches 0.8 (for most crops)

=== WATER-SAVING TECHNIQUES ===

1. ALTERNATE WETTING AND DRYING (AWD) for Rice:
   - Allow field to dry 15-20cm below surface before re-flooding
   - Saves 15-30% water with no yield loss
   - Insert a perforated pipe (30cm deep) to monitor water table

2. SYSTEM OF RICE INTENSIFICATION (SRI):
   - Single seedling per hill (vs. 3-4 in conventional)
   - Wider spacing: 25x25 cm
   - Intermittent irrigation (not continuous flooding)
   - Saves 25-50% water; yields increase 20-50%

3. ZERO TILLAGE (No-till/Conservation Agriculture):
   - Reduces soil evaporation
   - Conserves 15-20% moisture through residue retention

4. MULCHING:
   - Plastic mulch: 25-50 micron silver/black film reduces evaporation 60-70%
   - Organic mulch (straw, dry leaves): 10cm layer reduces soil temperature and moisture loss

5. LASER LAND LEVELING:
   - Saves 25-30% irrigation water through even distribution
   - Increases irrigation efficiency significantly

=== WATER QUALITY PARAMETERS ===
Good irrigation water should have:
  - pH: 6.5 - 8.5
  - EC: < 1.5 dS/m (marginal: 1.5-3.0; use tolerant crops above 3.0)
  - SAR (Sodium Adsorption Ratio): < 10 (above 18 is problematic)
  - Chloride: < 4 meq/L
  - Boron: < 1.0 mg/L

=== RAINWATER HARVESTING ===
Farm Pond:
  - Size: 0.04 ha (40m x 10m x 3m depth) serves 1 acre irrigation
  - Use: Life-saving irrigation during dry spells; 1-2 extra irrigations
  - Government: Mahatma Gandhi NREGA provides funding for farm ponds

Check Dams & Percolation Ponds:
  - Community-level water harvesting
  - Recharges groundwater; available for well/borewell irrigation

=== GOVERNMENT IRRIGATION SCHEMES ===
1. PMKSY (Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana): Drip/sprinkler subsidy
2. Har Khet Ko Pani: Expand irrigation coverage to unirrigated areas
3. Jal Jeevan Mission: Groundwater conservation
4. NABARD RIDF: Irrigation infrastructure financing for states
